# Meeting Talk Track — Thu Apr 30, 9:30am

*Linear script for rehearsal. Memorize the **structure** end-to-end so it becomes muscle memory. Memorize the lines marked **VERBATIM** literally. Everything else is a beat to hit, not words to recite.*

*Total budget: ~30 min. Opening = 90 sec. Demo = 5 min uninterrupted (8–10 with their questions). Q&A = the rest.*

---

## SECTION 1 — Opening (90 seconds) · **VERBATIM**

*Sit down. Set laptop on the table, lid still closed. Take out the printed PDFs.*

> "Thanks for taking the time."

*Hand each of them a printed overview PDF.*

> "I brought a one-pager — it might be useful to follow along, or for after."

*Brief beat.*

> "Quick context — Success at HS is a study-notes platform I built independently. The Sage instance is at sage.successaths.com. Students sign in with their `@sagehillschool.org` email and upload notes, study guides, or practice tests, organized by course and unit."

*Pause. Let it land.*

> "On academic integrity, there are three layers."
>
> "**One** — an honor-code checkbox at the moment of upload. Students confirm the material is their own work, or publicly shareable, before they can submit anything."
>
> "**Two** — every submission goes through an admin review queue. Nothing is visible to other students until a human admin has approved it."
>
> "**Three** — every approved item has a flag/report button, so any student can re-trigger review if something slips through."

*Pause again. This is the key moment.*

> "What I'm here to ask for today is permission for a four-week alpha — maybe 30 to 50 students between AP exams and finals — with the goal of a proper rollout in September if it goes well. Built and funded entirely by me. Sage bears no cost or technical responsibility."

*Genuine pause. Don't fill the silence.*

> "You don't owe me a yes today, and I'm flexible on whatever changes would make you both comfortable. Want me to walk you through the live site?"

***STOP TALKING. Wait for their response.***

*They will either say "go ahead" / "yes show us" or ask a question first. Both are fine. Answer any question they ask, then come back here when they're ready for the demo.*

---

## SECTION 2 — Demo transition (15 sec)

*When they say to proceed, open the laptop and turn it toward them.*

> "Okay, I'll keep this brief — about five minutes — and we can go deeper anywhere you want."

---

## SECTION 3 — Demo (5 minutes, six steps)

*The script below is what to say at each step. The exact words don't matter — hit the talking point and move on. If they interrupt, stop and answer; come back after.*

### Step 1 — Landing page (45 sec)

*Show `sage.successaths.com` (already loaded, signed out).*

**Talking point:** what a student sees logged out, plus the multi-tenant context.

> "This is what a student sees if they hit the site without an account. It's Sage-specific — every school we add gets its own version with their name. The platform itself is multi-tenant; Sage is one of the schools using it."

*Beat. Don't click yet — let them register the page.*

> "Let me sign in as a student."

### Step 2 — Sign in (30 sec)

*Click Sign In. Use the pre-loaded admin account.*

**Talking point:** restricted to verified school emails.

> "Sign-up and sign-in are restricted to `@sagehillschool.org` addresses. Anyone outside the domain bounces back. Everyone is identified."

*Sign in. Land on dashboard.*

### Step 3 — Browse → course → unit → material (75 sec)

**Talking point:** the student experience, organized by course and unit.

> "This is the dashboard — courses on the student's schedule. Or they can browse the full catalog…"

*Click Browse.*

> "…every department, every course Sage offers."

*Click into a course (e.g. AP Calculus BC).*

> "Each course has units."

*Click into a unit that has materials.*

> "Each unit has approved materials. Notes, PDFs, photo scans auto-converted to PDF, external links. Every one of these went through admin review."

*Click open a material.*

### Step 4 — Submit page · **THIS IS THE KEY STEP** (60 sec)

*Navigate to `/submit`.*

**Talking point:** the honor-code checkbox is the first integrity layer. Make sure they see it.

> "Now let's see what a student does to upload."

*Briefly show: pick course, pick unit, title, file picker.*

> "Pick a course, a unit, give it a title, drop the file."

*Point physically at the honor-code checkbox.*

> "And here — this is the first integrity layer. Required. Students can't submit without confirming the material is their own work or publicly shareable. Real tests, answer keys, copyrighted material — they're explicitly on the off-limits list."

*Brief pause. Make sure they LOOKED at it.*

> "Once they submit, it's invisible to other students until I approve it."

### Step 5 — Admin queue → approve (75 sec)

*Navigate to `/admin/submissions`.*

**Talking point:** every submission gets human review.

> "Which is where I come in. This is the admin moderation queue."

*Show the list of pending items.*

> "Every pending submission lands here. I can preview it, approve it, reject it with a note that goes back to the student, or edit it before approving."

*Click Preview on a realistic pending item.*

> "I see exactly what the student would see after approval, plus their identity. If it looks legitimate, I approve and it goes live. If it doesn't, I reject with a reason and they can revise."

*Approve it (or not — your choice).*

### Step 6 — Report / flag (45 sec)

*Navigate back to a unit page with approved materials.*

**Talking point:** the third moderation layer.

> "Third layer. Every approved material has this flag icon."

*Hover or click the flag.*

> "Any student can click it and report something — say it's a real test, or copyrighted, or anything. That triggers a re-review."

*Show the modal that opens.*

> "I see the flag immediately, can pull the material down with one click, and notify whoever's relevant on the school side."

*Close the modal.*

## SECTION 4 — Demo close (30 seconds) · **VERBATIM**

*Look up from the laptop. Eye contact with both of them. Slight pause.*

> "That's the platform. I won't show you the contest leaderboard or the marketing assets unless you want to dig into those — those are optional and off by default until the school approves anything."

*Beat. Open posture.*

> "What questions do you have?"

***STOP TALKING. Genuinely open question. Wait. Do not fill the silence.***

## SECTION 6 — Wrap (last 3–5 min) · key phrases **VERBATIM**

*When the conversation winds down, take control of the close.*

**Confirm the direction explicitly:**

> "To make sure I got the right summary — [paraphrase what they said]. Does that sound right?"

**Ask what they need from you:**

> "Is there anything specific you'd like me to come back with — a written privacy summary, a list of student admins, a faculty-sponsor proposal, anything for parent communication?"

**If they said yes (with or without conditions), confirm the asks:**

> "Three quick things on my end: a faculty admin contact name, whether there's any institutional channel you're comfortable announcing on — a newsletter mention, an email to seniors, whatever feels right — and an IT helpdesk push for `sage.successaths.com`."

*(The town meeting between now and end-of-year already passed, so don't ask for one. Keep the institutional ask flexible — newsletter / Dean's email / advisory-period mention — and let them pick the channel.)*

**Close:**

> "Thank you — I'll send a recap email this afternoon."

## RECOVERY LINES (in case you blank)

If you forget what comes next mid-meeting, any of these buy you a graceful pause:

> "Let me make sure I'm being clear about that…"
>
> "One second — let me find the right way to say this."
>
> "That's a fair concern. Let me think about it — what would a good answer look like to you?"
>
> "Can I take a note on that and come back to you tomorrow with a real answer?"
